# Figure 2. Successive fractionation steps reveal distinct layers of faecal chemistry

**a,** Processing workflow for the four-pool fractionation comparison. Counts indicate non-repeating annotations at each processing level or across the integrated analysis. **b,** Size-aware Venn and UpSet summary of annotation overlap among methanol crude, biphasic phases and fraction collections. Horizontal bars show processing-level totals and vertical bars show exact intersections. **c,** NPClassifier superclasses enriched among annotations unique to each processing level. Bubble size represents unique annotation count. **d,** Lineage-resolved comparison of a crude/reference sample, its matched primary-fraction collection, selected primary fractions 18 and 19, and the resulting C18 subfraction collection.

Twelve donor samples were combined into four biological pools. Detections were defined by `area > 0` and collapsed to one presence call per `annotation_id x biological pool x processing level`. Annotations were restricted to confidence levels 1-3, with MSNovelist and placeholder annotations removed. For panel **d**, positive- and negative-ion datasets were merged within each collection and counted independently.
