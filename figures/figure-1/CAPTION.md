# Figure 1. Construction and composition of the human faecal fraction atlas

**a,** Workflow used to generate the fraction-resolved faecal metabolome atlas from crude extracts, biphasic phases, primary fractions and subfractions. **b,** Circular lineage tree linking deidentified donor aliases, human samples (`HGMH`), extracts (`HGME`) and fractions (`HGMF`) across the 140 retained datasets. Fraction nodes are coloured by inferred fractionation type. **c,** Non-repeating annotations at confidence levels 1-3, stacked by annotation evidence source. **d,** Nested chemical-class composition of atlas annotations with NPClassifier support. The inner ring shows the ten most abundant named superclasses and the outer ring shows major subclasses. **e,** Platform-resolved coverage across the 30 most abundant matched NPClassifier classes. Side bars show total and platform-unique annotation counts using all retained level 1-3 annotations.

Annotations were filtered to confidence levels 1-3. MSNovelist annotations and analogue, candidate and PubChem-placeholder names were removed. Non-repeating annotations were counted by `annotation_id` (compound name where available, otherwise SMILES). Panel **d** counts each non-empty SMILES once before NPClassifier matching, whereas panel **e** counts annotations within each `column.type x ion.mode` platform.
