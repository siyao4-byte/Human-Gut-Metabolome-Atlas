# Figure 3. Fractionation reveals pool-level and individual metabolite variability masked in crude profiles

**a,** Unsupervised clustering of twelve individual crude profiles using the original pellet-normalized Bray-Curtis donor-distance matrix. Metadata bars show pool, diet, gender and ethnicity. **b,** PCA of pool-aggregated crude profiles after merging individual donor results within each pool and crude phase. **c,** PCA of fraction-resolved profiles across pool, fraction type and fraction sequence. Ellipses show 80% normal intervals for each `pool x fraction type` group where sufficient points were available. **d,e,** Volcano plots comparing Pool 1 and Pool 4 in crude (**d**) and fractionated (**e**) profiles, with adjacent bubble plots showing the ten strongest significant changes. Bubble position reports log2 fold change and bubble size reports `-log10(BH-adjusted FDR)`. **f,** Common, shared and pool-unique annotations after combining crude and fraction detections, faceted by confidence level. **g,** NPClassifier superclass composition of common annotations detected in all four pools.

Ordinations used unsupervised PCA (`stats::prcomp`). Differential abundance used `limma::lmFit` and `limma::eBayes`, with BH-adjusted FDR control. Quantitative data were median-normalized within dataset and transformed as `log10(normalized area + 1)`. Presence summaries collapse repeated detections to one call per `annotation_id x pool`.
